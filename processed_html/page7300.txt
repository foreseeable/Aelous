The Virginia Zoo opens up the new World of Reptiles exhibit officially opens up on Monday, June 4, 2018. If youre lucky, a smiling visitor will greet you when you walk into the Virginia Zoos latest exhibit, World of Reptiles. Inside the revamped building, a glass-walled exhibit is first up on your left-hand side, with a grays monitor, a large, muscular-looking lizard. Grays monitors are kept in just a handful of zoos in the country, and this one is the only one on the East Coast. The lizard may be lounging on a branch in his exhibit, slyly smiling as he did when guests got a sneak peek of the building earlier this month. Or he might be munching on fruit, which he would be doing in the wild in his native land of the Philippines. Either way, hes one of the more than 200 residents making homes out of the 65 exhibits in the redone and rebuilt World of Reptiles, the zoos latest attraction designed to entertain and educate visitors about the world around them. The Virginia Zoo opens up the new World of Reptiles exhibit officially opens up on Monday, June 4, 2018. The Zoo to Do Gala was this past Saturday and some people got a sneek-peak at the exhibit. The $3.6 million makeover  which includes construction and renovations, animal acquisitions, exhibit buildouts, and technology  opened June 4, although guests at the zoos annual Zoo To Do fundraising party got an early look on June 2. The building closed in November 2016 for the renovations, which largely kept the same footprint of the original 1974 building. Now that its reopened, guests can learn more about conservation efforts, observe a tropical frogs metamorphosis first-hand in the amphibian breeding facility and see itty-bitty newborn reptiles in the glass-walled nursery. A wing of venomous snakes will highlight the diversity of snakes, from the small, bright yellow eyelash viper  so named for its bright yellow scaly eyelashes  to two big, hulking reticulated pythons. Carnivorous green, blue, black and yellow cousins of the fruit-loving grays monitor aim to show the diversity of the types of lizards. I think people have been wanting to see the building open again and renovated with all the new exhibits, said John Wyatt, a herpetology keeper at the zoo. Its been down for awhile, so people were anxious to see all the new species that were going to have in here. Its been exciting. I like so much of the stuff that we have in here. I think all the monitor species that we have are really, really cool. I think people will like that because we have so many different types. Some animals have yet to move in. Three two-toed sloths and pairs of Saki and Titi monkeys are behind-the-scenes, getting acclimated and finishing required quarantine periods before theyll be on exhibit near the exit of the building. The monkeys have outside access, too, with an area for guests to observe training sessions in the future. A new aquatic gallery, part of a partnership with the Elizabeth River Project, still has to add its seahorses, Iranian newts and several other residents. An alligator snapping turtle already menaces in a corner of that wing. The Daily Press was on hand Saturday night as the Virginia Zoo celebrated its newest exhibit, World of Reptiles, with its annual Zoo To Do party. A big highlight of the revitalization is the new Crocodile Marsh, which adds 850 square feet to the 13,000-square-foot building. The zoo hopes to add a male at a future date. Floor-to-ceiling glass windows will give brave guests an up-close view of the girls once theyre swimming in their pond. Zoo marketing manager Ashley Mars said the plan is for the aquatic gallery animals to move in early next week, and the crocodiles, sloths and monkeys will be out by the end of June. By the end of the year, another 2,050-square-foot addition, the Tropical Conservatory, will open for more mammals, birds and reptiles. Guests to the Zoo To Do party were encouraged to dress up in honor of the new exhibits, with many indulging the theme in scaly-patterned attire and, often, tattoos of reptiles and amphibians. Gene Parker and Ed Credle each donned matching crocodile heads and tails, with tuxedos in-between. Its very cool, very nice. I think the zoo has really come up a lot, Parker said, eyeing the enclosure where the crocodiles will roam. This is so much nicer.
